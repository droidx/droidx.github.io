    Smartphone controlled Robotic System as Assistive Technology

    The smartphone is essentially mounted on an electric wheelchair. It takes input commands from the user by touch (uses the touchscreen) or the head-movements (uses the camera). Both this input commands are mapped to the speed and the direction of the motors. Additionally, a usability study was conducted where the prototype was validated by seven healthy participants.[pdf]

    Caltech Community Seismic Network

    The Community Seismic Network Project at Caltech is a distributed Sensor Network of accelerometer sensor for implementing early warning and optimum damage mitigation systems for earthquakes. I developed a robust client for the Community Seismic Network on Android Platform. This client interfaces a low-cost seismic sensor, collects the sensor data and sends it to the cloud to identify potential earthquakes. I worked on this as a part of Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowship at California Institute of Technology during my Junior year. [code]

    Classification of Physiological signals into cognitive emotional states using Machine Learning

    I developed a Machine Learning Model that recognizes human affective states based on different physiological signals comprising of ECG, PPG, SKT, GSR and Electrodermal activity. The main objective was to evaluate affective states recognition in typically developing individuals as well as those with Autism Spectral Disorder (ASD). [pdf]
