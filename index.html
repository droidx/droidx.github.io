    Skim - Flipboard for Adventures

    Skim brings awesome curated adventures in your city to your phone every week. You can skim through our suggested adventures, ping you friends, meet and make memories! Skim is like a Flipboard for Adventures! So you can just go out there and experience a life outside work. Skim is available for 6 cities, Los Angeles, San Francisco, New York, London, Paris, Bangalore.

    Shortr - Tech Blogs, News, Tutorials in 50 words

    Shortr is a mobile app that shows tech news, blogs and tutorials in 50 words. Users can follow specific topics in tech. The app, currently in beta, covers specific topics like AngularJS, NodeJS, Engineering Blogs, etc. The app also has a Top News feed that covers the top tech news summaries across all topics. Shortr is ideal for people who want to stay updated with niche topics in tech while saving time!

    bookOverflow - Reminder for library books for IITGN students

    I built this app in my Junior year for the students of IIT Gandhinagar. I realized that many of us including me would forget to return our library books on time and consequently shell out money as late fees. So, I built this app which just required an IITGN student to put in the roll no and the app would automatically sync all the books issued and create a reminder when the books are due. The app was hosted on college's local server. [code] [blog post]

    Caltech Community Seismic Network

    The Community Seismic Network Project at Caltech is a distributed Sensor Network of accelerometer sensor for implementing early warning and optimum damage mitigation systems for earthquakes. I developed a robust client for the Community Seismic Network on Android Platform. This client interfaces a low-cost seismic sensor, collects the sensor data and sends it to the cloud to identify potential earthquakes. I worked on this as a part of Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowship at California Institute of Technology during my Junior year. [code]

    Classification of Physiological signals into cognitive emotional states using Machine Learning

    I developed a Machine Learning Model that recognizes human affective states based on different physiological signals comprising of ECG, PPG, SKT, GSR and Electrodermal activity. The main objective was to evaluate affective states recognition in typically developing (TD) individuals as well as those with Autism Spectral Disorder (ASD). The algorithm developed was able to achieve an accuracy of 80% for the TD individuals. [pdf]
